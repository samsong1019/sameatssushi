Best Sushi for Beginners

If you are new to sushi, it can be initmidating. But don't worry start off with something harmless like a california roll, or a shrimp tempura roll. Most of the time these don't contain any raw food so you wont have to worry about that. They aren't my personal fav or even close but they are there to open you up to the best cuisine.

Nigiri vs. Sashimi

Here is where sushi really shines, Nigiri and Sashimi my personal favs. The difference between these two is rice. Nigiri is my personal favorite most of the time. Nigiri has fish and a ball of rice. that simple. Sashimi is my second favorite but also my favorite most of the time haha, sashmimi is just the fish alone. No rice, no nothing just fish. Now you know the difference between Nigiri and Sashimi

My Top Picks

My favorite types of nigiri is Otoro, Salmon belly, and UNI. Otoro is like the wagyu of the sea, you bite into it and imediately the fat from the blue fin tuna start melting and covering every area of your mouth and as you chew, you get more and more of that oil and you notice it coats each individual grain of rice. The texture is immaculate and to die for, and to put everything together the salt from the soysauce brings out the beauty from the fish and wasabit makes everything balanced. Then we have Salmon Belly, this is bar FAR the best part of the Salmon. This is nice and rich and because of the fat it has an interesting firmness to it that disapates as you begin to chew. The sweetness from the salmon matches so well with the wasabi and the flavor is brought out by the soy sauce. Lastly we have the UNI. UNI is sea urchin the prickly un edible looking things in the sea. They are in fact very edible, (or some of them) Uni can be so so so sweet and creamy, its amazing. with just some sushi rice and a bit of nori to bring out some umami flavors tied together with a splash of soy sauce to bring out the sweetness of the uni. truly a masterpiece.
